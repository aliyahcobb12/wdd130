Silent Study Spots at BYU-Idaho

David O. McKay Library

The David O. McKay Library has a variety of areas for study. This particular spot is found on the bottom floor of the library. It is almost always completely quiet, making it the perfect place to sit and study with zero distractions

The BYU-Idaho I-Center is one of the most intersting buildings on campus, often hosting some of the greatest events on the center stage. However, during the busy school hours, when no events are being held, the building houses 3 levels of comfortable arm chairs and virtually no noise or traffic. No classes are held in this building, making it perfect for finding a quiet place to focus.

The Romney Building on campus has some of the most facinating artwork and displays on campus. There are plentyof infomational graphics and old artifacts to occupy a study break. However, it is also one of the quietest buildings, and if your lucky enough to snag a booth, it is one of the best buildings to study in with little noise to distract.
